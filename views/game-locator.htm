Need Help?

Please follow these steps to help your customer locate a specific game

  1. 1. Check the Sports/RSN Lookup tool

    If a customer is having trouble finding a game, check the Sports/RSN Lookup tool to find out when and what channel the game is on.

    Tips

    The Sports/RSN Lookup tool displays channel information for RSN, PPV or Sports Subscription games.

    The tool is updated daily and even displays replay information in case the customer missed a game the first time.

    Related Links

    • DIRECTV.com Channel Listings

  2. 2. Check local channels

    A game may be on: Locals, RSNs and National and Alternate channels.

    For local channels:

    1. Have the customer check for the game on local stations. See the Local Availability tool.
    2. Have customer check for the game on the local RSN. See the Sports/RSN Lookup tool.

    Tips

    If the team isn't in the RSN tool:

    • Team is out of the customer's area.
    • Team has no RSN coverage.
    • Team is for a sport not in the RSN tool (NFL, college).
    • DIRECTV may no longer have rights to broadcast the game.

    Related Links

    • Professional Teams with No RSN

  4. 4. Check alternate channels

    Alternate channels are usually found on channels near the customer's local RSN or channels like ESPN or ESPN2.

    Alternate channels can be found if more than one professional game of the same sport is played at the same time on the same channel. An alternate channel my open up and carry one of the games.

    Tips

    Customers can't subscribe to these channels and they're only viewable while the game is in progress.

    Related Links

    • Alternate Channels

  5. 5. Sports subscription blackouts

    If a customer has a sports subscription, specific blackout rules may apply to each individual sport. They are different for each package.

    Tips

    Check to see what sports subscription the customer has and reference the appropriate blackout section on the corresponding Agent Answer Center page.

    Related Links

  6. 6. Offer a sports subscription as a possible solution

    If you´ve checked all possible channels and specific blackout rules for the sport or team, and still can't locate the game, you might want to offer a sports subscription if the customer doesn't already have one.

    Sports subscriptions allow fans to watch teams outside their local areas, follow their favorite teams across the country, watch games normally not seen and see more games of their favorite sport.

    Tips

    If a customer's favorite team is not the local team it's considered an "out-of-market" team. Sports subscriptions are great additions for fans of non-local teams. Ask the customer if the team he/she wants to see is a non-local (out-of-market) team.

    Related Links

    • PPV College Football

  7. 7. Customer is completely blacked out

    If you still can't locate the customer's game on any of the channels listed previously, it may be blacked out in the area completely.

    Games are blacked out to protect the teams, the sports leagues and the stations that purchase the rights to broadcast individual games.

    Explain that DIRECTV must follow blackout guidelines provided by the leagues, sports associations and program carriers.

    Customers in certain areas will not be able to view games on the RSN or in the league package. Check individual RSN pages and NBA, NHL, and MLB Blackout pages for more information.

    Tips

    Explain: "Sports blackouts are controlled by the leagues, sports associations, and program carriers. In some areas, DIRECTV may no longer have rights to broadcast the customer's games."

    "Thank you for your patience. I understand how frustrating this can be. We would be happy to check with our broadcast center to ensure there are no broadcasting issues."

    See Response to Blackouts in Agent Answer Center.

  8. 8. Blackout is incorrect

    If you have completed all troubleshooting instructions, and you still can't locate the customer's game on any of the channels listed previously, and you feel that the blackout is in error, escalate the issue to a Team Leader.

    Say "Thank you for your patience. I understand how frustrating this can be. We would be happy to check with our broadcast center to ensure there are no broadcasting issues."
